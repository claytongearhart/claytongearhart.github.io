Polynomials

A polynomial is an expression consisting of variables, coefficients, and the operations of addition, subtraction, multiplication, and non-negative integer exponentiation of variables. An example of a polynomial is \(x^2 − 4x + 7\). A term is a section of a polynomial consisting only of a single coefficient, variable, and exponent. In the above example, \(x^2 − 4x + 7\), the terms are \(x^2\), \(-4x\), and \(7\). A polynomial with only one term is called a monomial, polynomials with two and three terms are called binomials and trinomials respectively.

The degree of a term is the power that it's variable is raised to, and the degree of an entire polynomial is the degree of it's highest degree term. The roots of a polynomial are the input values that yield an output of zero, these roots can be imaginary.

Factorization

All polynomials can be written as a factored form in which the polynomial is written as the product of irreducible polynomials. To factor a polynomial we find the greatest common factor of all the coefficients, and then notate it as the factor times the polynomial divided by that factor. As an example, let us factor the polynomial \(6x^2 + 12x - 9\). \(3\) is the greatest factor that the coefficients: \(6\), \(12\), and \(9\) share. Knowing this we can rewrite the polynomial as \(3(2x^2 + 6x - 3)\). It should also be noted that you can factor out variables as well. As a quick example: \(4x^3 + 8x\) factors to \(4x(x^2 + 2)\).

Quadratics

A quadratic is a 2nd degree polynomial. The standard quadratic form is \(ax^2 + bx + c\). We can find the roots of a quadratic using the quadratic formula, \(\frac{-b\pm\sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}\). When \(a = 1\), or is factored out the two roots of a polynomial, \(r_1\) and \(r_2\), satisfy the identity An equations that shows how two expressions are related. \(r_1 + r_2 = b\) and \(r_1 \cdot r_2 = c\).

Radicals & Roots

An \(n\)th root of a number \(x\) is a number \(r\) which, when raised to the power \(n\), yields \(x\): \(r^n = x\).

Rationals

A rational expression is an expression consisting of one polynomial divided by another. A rational equation is an equation of rational expressions. In order to solve rational equations often times you must factor the denominators as to create a common denominator. You can factor both the numerator and denominator to find common factors, which can be removed as they cancel out.

Exponentials

For any real number \(a\), and any real number \(n\), \(a^n\) is the \(a\) multiplied by itself \(n\) times. In this circumstance \(a\) is the base and \(n\) is the exponent. Listed below are some properties of operations on exponents with a common base.

  • \(x^m \cdot x^n = x^{m+n}\)
  • \(\frac{x^m}{x^n} = x^{m-n}\)
  • \((x^m)^n = x^{m \cdot n}\)
  • \(x^0 = 1\)

Exponential Functions

An exponential function is a function that takes the general form \(f(x) = ab^x\). Exponential function are used to model exponential growth, which is when the output values changes by the same ratio between every equal distant set of input points.

Logarithms

A logarithm is the inverse operation to exponentiation. If \(x^n = m\), then \(\log_x(m)=n\). This identity allows us to solve both exponential, and logarithmic problems. For example, if it's given that \(2^{3x} = 64\), the value of \(3x\) can be calculated as \(\log_2(64) = 6\), then divide by three to get the value of \(x\) as two.
